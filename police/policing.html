Comparing Policing Outcomes by District

Let's explore policing outcomes in different policing districts in Denver. Using a policing dataset from the month of November 2012, we can look at the percentage of police stops that ended in arrest, and police stops that ended with just a warning. Here is an image showing the different police districts.

Percentage of Stops Ending with a Warning

The chart below shows how many police stops (vehicle and pedestrian) ended up with just a warning being issued. Each bar represents a defferent police districts.

Analysis

The two charts offer interesting insights. it appears there are clearly districts where arrests are more likely to occur during police stops, primarily district 6, Downtown and district 2, the East Colfax/5 Points area. Downtown, 18% of stops ended with an arrest, compared to 11% in the far more affluent Cherry Creek/Wash Park area. Even more interesting, if you look at police stops that ended with the individual being let off with a warning, we see a different story. A mere 5% of stops occuring downtown end with a warning, but in the more affluent area of Cherry Creek/Wash Park, we see 15% of police stops ending with the individual being let off on a warning.

It's hard to look at these charts and not come to the conclusion that more affluent areas are policed more leniently than areas that have a higher homeless/low income population. However, more research would need to be done to soundly come to this conclusion. For example, it would be interesting to add and compare average income with this analysis to gain additional insights. Also, we could look further into the individual districts and investigate arrest rates in the individual precincts that make up each district.
