a quick overview of life lost during the LA riots

Following the aquittal of 4 LAPD officers that nearly beat Rodney King to death, unrest broke out in the streets of Los Angeles in 1992. 64 people were killed.

Life Lost by Race

The chart below breaks down lives lost by race

Interpretation: The data shows that out of the 64 people killed, Black individuals were killed most during the riots, followed by Latinos.

Cause of Death by Race

This chart breaks down the cause of death for each race

Interpretation: Upon further inspection of the data, we see that homicides carried out by other people (not police related) are distributed fairly equally across white, latino, and black individuals. We can also see that only black and latino individuals were killed by police involved shootings. No white or asian individuals were killed by police officers.

Visualization Tutorial

Scroll through code below to see how the above visualization was made. Y axis was again aggregated to show totals of deaths by race. A facet was added to this visualization to break down the cause of death by each race, instead of just the totals. A "facet" was added by specifying the "column" property, then the field is specified to indicate the value from the dataset. 


						...
						"view":{"fill":"lightgrey"},
						"width":500,
						"height":300,
						"mark": "bar",
						"encoding": {
							"x": {
								"title":"Race of Deceased",
								"axis":{
									"labelFontSize":15
								},
								"field": "race",
								"type":"nominal"
							},
							"y": {
								"aggregate": "count",
								"title":"Number of Deaths",
								"axis":{
									"labelFontSize":15
								},
								"field": "type",
								"type":"nominal"
							},
							"column": {
								"field": "type",
								"type": "quantitative",
								"bin": {
									"maxbins": 15
